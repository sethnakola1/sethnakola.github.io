Privacy Policy

Last Updated:

DrCareAI is committed to protecting the privacy, confidentiality, and security of patient and user data. We align with Indian regulatory expectations (including DISHA draft principles and DPDP Act intents) and international best practices (ISO 27001). This policy explains how we collect, use, disclose, and protect information.

1. Information We Collect

  • Identity and Contact: name, email, phone number when you contact us.
  • Health and Medical: symptoms, diagnoses, medications, orders, reports as provided by your provider or you.
  • Usage and Device: IP address, device identifiers, app interactions, and analytics events.
  • Operational Records: appointments, billing, claims, and audit logs relevant to care delivery.

2. How We Use Information

  • Care Delivery: provide services, generate documentation, eRx, discharge summaries, and notifications.
  • Quality & Safety: clinical decision support, alerts, and workflow optimization.
  • Operations: billing, inventory, claims processing, and reconciliation.
  • Product Improvement: de-identified analytics to improve accuracy, reliability, and performance.
  • Legal & Compliance: maintain audit trails, consent records, and respond to lawful requests.

3. Sharing and Disclosure

  • With Providers/Partners involved in your care, with appropriate consent and safeguards.
  • With Service Providers under contracts that require confidentiality and security.
  • For Compliance or Lawful Requests where we are legally required to do so.
  • We do not sell personal data.

4. Security

  • Encryption in transit and at rest; key management best practices.
  • Role-based access, SSO, MFA, IP allow-listing, and least-privilege enforcement.
  • Backups, disaster recovery, and continuous monitoring with vulnerability management.

5. Data Retention

  • We retain data only as long as necessary for care, operations, and compliance.
  • De-identified data may be retained for analytics and research.

6. International Transfers

Data may be processed in India and other jurisdictions with appropriate safeguards.

7. Your Rights

  • Access, correction, deletion (subject to legal requirements), and data portability.
  • Withdraw consent where processing is based on consent.
  • Lodge complaints with applicable authorities.
