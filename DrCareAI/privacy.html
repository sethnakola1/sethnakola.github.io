DrCareAI Privacy Policy

AI-Powered Healthcare Platform | A Product of AIConnect Hub

Effective Date: October 24, 2025

Last Updated: October 24, 2025

1. Introduction

DrCareAI ("we," "our," or "us") is a cloud-based, multi-tenant healthcare SaaS platform developed by AIConnect Hub. We are committed to protecting the privacy and security of personal health information (PHI) and personal data of our users, including healthcare providers, patients, and authorized personnel.

This Privacy Policy outlines our practices regarding the collection, use, storage, and protection of information through our platform, in compliance with applicable healthcare privacy laws including HIPAA (Health Insurance Portability and Accountability Act), DPDP (Digital Personal Data Protection) Act 2023, and other relevant data protection regulations.

2. Information We Collect

2.1 Patient Health Information (PHI)

  • Medical records, diagnoses, and treatment history
  • Prescription and medication data
  • Laboratory and diagnostic test results
  • Biometric and vital sign data
  • Insurance and billing information
  • Emergency contact information

2.2 User Account Information

  • Healthcare provider credentials and licensing information
  • Staff member access credentials and role-based permissions
  • Hospital and clinic operational data
  • Usage analytics and system interaction logs

2.3 Technical Information

  • Device information and IP addresses
  • Browser type and operating system
  • Access timestamps and audit trails
  • System performance and error logs

3. How We Use Your Information

3.1 Healthcare Operations

  • Facilitating patient care and clinical decision-making
  • AI-assisted diagnostic forecasting and treatment recommendations
  • Pharmacy fulfillment and e-prescription management
  • Billing and insurance claim processing
  • Hospital operations and workflow optimization

3.2 AI and Machine Learning

Our AI systems analyze de-identified and aggregated health data to:

  • Improve diagnostic accuracy and treatment outcomes
  • Detect patterns and anomalies in patient data
  • Enhance clinical decision support systems
  • Optimize hospital resource allocation

AI Transparency: All AI-generated recommendations include explainability features, allowing healthcare providers to understand the reasoning behind AI suggestions. No treatment decisions are made solely by AI without human oversight.

4. Data Storage and Security

4.1 Cloud Infrastructure

  • Data is stored on HIPAA-compliant cloud infrastructure with industry-leading security measures
  • All data transmission is encrypted using TLS 1.3 or higher
  • Data at rest is encrypted using AES-256 encryption
  • Multi-factor authentication (MFA) required for all user accounts
  • Regular security audits and penetration testing

4.2 Access Controls

  • Role-based access control (RBAC) limiting data access to authorized personnel only
  • Comprehensive audit logs tracking all data access and modifications
  • Automatic session timeout and secure logout procedures
  • Regular access reviews and permission updates

5. Data Sharing and Disclosure

5.1 Permitted Disclosures

We may share protected health information only in the following circumstances:

  • With patient's explicit written consent
  • For treatment, payment, and healthcare operations as permitted by HIPAA
  • When required by law or court order
  • For public health activities and reporting
  • To prevent serious threat to health or safety

5.2 Third-Party Service Providers

We work with HIPAA-compliant third-party service providers for:

  • Cloud hosting and infrastructure services
  • Payment processing (Stripe, UPI gateway providers)
  • SMS and email notification services
  • Analytics and performance monitoring

All third-party providers sign Business Associate Agreements (BAAs) ensuring HIPAA compliance.

6. Compliance and Regulations

6.1 HIPAA Compliance

  • Comprehensive administrative, physical, and technical safeguards
  • Employee training on HIPAA privacy and security requirements
  • Incident response procedures for potential breaches
  • Regular compliance audits and assessments

6.2 DPDP Act 2023 Compliance

  • Lawful basis for processing personal data
  • Data minimization and purpose limitation
  • Individual rights including access, correction, and deletion
  • Data localization requirements for Indian users
  • Consent management and withdrawal mechanisms

7. Data Retention and Disposal

7.1 Retention Periods

  • Patient Medical Records: Retained as per local healthcare regulations (typically 7-10 years)
  • Audit Logs: Maintained for 6 years minimum
  • Billing Records: Retained for 7 years
  • System Logs: Retained for 2 years

7.2 Secure Disposal

Upon expiration of retention periods, data is securely deleted using industry-standard methods including cryptographic erasure and physical destruction of storage media.

8. Individual Rights

8.1 Patient Rights

Under applicable privacy laws, individuals have the right to:

  • Access their personal health information
  • Request corrections to inaccurate information
  • Request restrictions on use and disclosure
  • Request alternative forms of communication
  • File complaints regarding privacy practices
  • Data portability (transfer to another provider)

8.2 How to Exercise Rights

To exercise these rights or make inquiries about your personal information, please contact our Privacy Officer using the information provided below.

9. Breach Notification

In the unlikely event of a data breach involving personal health information, we will:

  • Notify affected individuals within 60 days of discovery
  • Report to relevant authorities within required timeframes
  • Provide details about the breach and steps being taken
  • Offer appropriate assistance and remediation

10. Updates to This Policy

We may update this Privacy Policy from time to time to reflect changes in our practices, technology, or applicable laws. We will notify users of material changes through our platform and by updating the "Last Updated" date above.
